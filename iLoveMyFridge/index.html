Application Goals

I love my fridge is a web based responsive application, it is designed and developed to help families keeping track of things they store in their fridge. It is very common for us to purchase food and after forget about the expiration date. A common consequence is getting rid of food we did not consumed on time, and at the same time, losing money for something we never used. Our mind, does not, and cannot not remember the expiration date of each product we purchased. The goal of I love my fridge is to allow us to store products adding their expiration date, and being reminded when one of our product is about to expire. Its objective is helping us avoiding wasting food, save money, and help our planet reducing the amount of garbage produce daily.

The main target for this application is most probably a woman between 30 and 45 years old. She can be single with a brilliant career and a very busy an intense life made of sports and social activities as well as an housewife always on top of modern solutions that allow her to save money and improving her quality of life as well as the quality of life of her family members.

Application Tasks

List of tasks performed by the application owner:

  • The user will install the app either from the Gooogle Play or Apple Store.
    • Note: The user can create more than one fridge
  • Once the app is launched the user will be ask to register providing basic information such as:
    • User's first name
    • User's last name
    • A username in a form of an email address
    • A password
  • When the registration is completed the user's information will be stored in his/her database profile as well as in the local application storage for easy access during the following sessions.
  • Once the authentication process is completed the user will be able to use the application.
  • Now the user can create a Fridge (Container) and once it is created, he/she can start adding products to the fridge.
  • The use click on the add product button and inserts the following date:
    • Product name – description
    • Product expiration date
  • When the product description and product expiration date have been completed the user can save it in the selected fridge
    • Note: The product will be also saved in the general products database table in order to allow the user to easily search for it next time
  • The "add product" can be also done using the scan barcode button. It allows the app owner to scan the barcode of a product and retrieve product’s information that can be added to the fridge and that will be also stored in the food database.
  • The application will monitor the product expiration date of each product and notify the user when a specific product is about to expire.
  • When a specific product expires, the app owner will be also notified and the product will be automatically removed from the fridge.

Application Comparison

When I started thinking about my app idea, I did not know whether there was already a similar solution on the Apple App Store or Google Play store. Finally, I found an app in the Apple store that does what my original idea is supposed to do at the end of this module.

In this section, we are going to analyse and compare them. The app I have used as reference for this project is called NoWaste and Kasper Hjortsballe developed it.

The aim of NoWaste is to easily track, organize and manage the food in your home. Let us now analyse NoWaste compared to I Love my Fridge.

NoWaste Features

  • Users can easily see the inventory lists for his/her freezer, fridge and pantry.
  • Users can sort your food by expiration date, name or category.
  • Users can search and find food and the application provides with a large amount of products in the database.
  • The application also allows the user to synchronize and share his/her lists with their families.
  • Users can easily check the monthly food waste & savings.
  • The application implements a community feature that allows the app owner to share and see community user’s results.
  • Users can scan the barcode of real products and easily add them to the database.

NoWaste Improvements

Despite the fact that the application is functionally easy to understand and provides with all the capabilities the final user would like to have, there are things that could be improved. For instance, to my eyes, the UX of the app is not the very user-friendly. The menu option is not a common component shared across iOS or Android apps and it is not very intuitive for a brand new user.

In addition, there is not any form of notifications from the application when a product is going to expire. If the user does not open the app, he/her is not notified by the application that a product is near its expiration date. As a user, I have to manually open the app, check the list of my fridges and manually remove specific products. In my option the app should inform me that a product is about to expire first, and when the product itself is expired the app should notify me again that the product is now removed from my fridge because it is expired.

I love my Fridge Features

A premise should be made before comparing the two applications. I Love my Fridge would be a prototype of a mobile responsive app that perform similar tasks compared to NoWaste. Having said that thinking that my final prototype will be as good as NoWaste in just few weeks of design and development would not be fair.

What I would like to improve is the way the app inform the users about product expirations date and create a more user-friendly experience for the user.

Below the list of functionalities I would like to implement for the final submission deadline:

  • A database that allows the user to store products and their expiration dates:
    • Note: The database will not be populated with standard products, it will be empty, but the system will save products inserted by the user automatically.
  • A checking system to control product deadlines.
  • A notification system to inform, at least by emails, the user about product expiration dates and capable of removing expired products from a specific fridge.
  • A more intuitive UI to facilitate common user interactions with the application.

Application Challenges

Below the list of technological challenges, I will face during the design and development of I Love my Fridge:

Data Persistence


They first challenge we have to face is how data is persisted during a session. We want to be able to store authentication credentials after the first registration/login phase, avoiding to always asking the user for email and password. We could rely on the local storage, being our application a web-based solution. However storing user’s credentials in the local storage is not a security best practice. In order to solve this issue, when a user complete the registration process, our application could generate a token that will be associated to the user’s profile and stored as a key to remember his/her credentials. Therefore storing the token on the local storage, we can retrieve it when the application is launched and compared it with the persistent data stored on the user’s database profile to perform a match and automatically authenticate our user. At the same time, we do not expose any user’s sensitive information in the local storage.

Another limitation of the local storage solution is related to the amount of data and it relationship. Not only the user can store many products in their fridges, but we also have to manage several 1-to-N data relationships in order to provide with the application functionalities we would love to achieve.

A more complex data structure is required and therefore the application will require a database component that will be used to store user and products information.

Application Architecture


The nature of our application automatically excludes the use of a native database driver that can be used and deploy inside the same bundle. For instance, the Sqlite Database and the sqlite3 C-library available in iOS.

For a web-based application, we need to store data on a different middleware component. This consideration bring us to consider designing a three-tiers application, in which the client side is represented by our mobile web app. It will be also require the design and development of a middleware component that will exposes RestFul endpoints that can be consumed by our mobile client. The middleware tier would then communicate directly with our database layer to retrieve and store user’s data, based on the event raised by our mobile client.

Additional Application Functionalities


We would love to be able to allow the user to scan products using the mobile camera available in their smartphones. This is something that requires the use of plugins that facilitate the interaction with the hardware components of the mobile device.

The use of a framework that can help reducing this complexity is required.
